Opportunity: Online Communications Manager (part-time)

To apply for this position, please send your CV and cover letter to contact@govright.org. 

GovRight is seeking a passionate individual to play a leading and highly visible role in how our organization represents itself and its work to the public. You will be responsible for the public face of our organization on social media, our website and more. This role is a ground-floor position in a small organization with high growth potential.

GovRight is a small, agile, social enterprise specializing in technology and communication tools to enable activists, organizations, and governments to better engage citizens in the process of government and particularly legislation. Our work takes a highly international focus and our expertise lies in solving difficult problems in complex regions of the world. We are supported by a number of leading donors and partner organizations, including The United States Institute for Peace, World Resources Institute, Cornell University, and Mundiapolis University.

Responsibilities:

You will be responsible for the implementation of our organization’s online communications strategy. Core tasks and activities shall include:

  • Bi-weekly blog posts written on topics core to our organization and its operations. We will assist with the content for these posts, but expect them to be written up in a highly-professional manner.
  • Weekly posts to social media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram) about our organization, its partners, and related activities.
  • Interaction with followers and fans on social media (as appropriate).

Secondary tasks will include (as time permits),

  • Management of our public email contact and correspondence.
  • Publication and maintenance of website material.

Growth Opportunities:

You will enter this role as the sole member of our communications team. Success and motivation in this role will lead to an increasingly public and critical role in communications for our organization, which may include:

  • Professional writing opportunities, including white papers and grant proposals.
  • Donor and partner organization relationship management.
  • Community development and evangelism, including both online and at events/conferences/trade shows.
  • Media relations.

Workload Expectations:

The starting workload expected for this role is only 5-15 hours per month. We intend to expand the role with evidence of success and as the scope of our operations increase over the next three to six months.

Requirements:

  • Exceptional English-language writing skills, with experience writing in a professional or academic setting. This is a very strict requirement.
  • Experience with and enthusiasm for online communication, particularly via social media.
  • Desired: Additional language skills.
  • Desired: Work experience in legal, technology, or civil society contexts.

Compensation & Benefits:

  • Hourly compensation (based on experience and market rate).
  • Fully remote with flexible scheduling.
  • Public role with direct, personal recognition.

 

To apply for this position, please send your CV and cover letter to contact@govright.org.
